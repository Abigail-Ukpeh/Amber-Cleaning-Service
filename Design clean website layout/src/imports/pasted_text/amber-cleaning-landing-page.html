Design a clean, professional, high-converting one-page landing page for "Amber's Cleaning Service," a residential and commercial cleaning business. The brand logo (already designed) features playful hand-lettered pink script text with a soft teal/mint swirl behind it and small sparkle/confetti accents in pink, teal, and burgundy. Use this logo as the foundation for the entire site's color palette and pull the design together around it. Even though the logo itself is whimsical, the website should feel polished, premium, and trustworthy, not childish, so balance the playful brand color with a lot of clean white space and confident typography. Color palette (pulled directly from the logo): Primary pink/magenta (#E8438C or similar vibrant pink) for headlines, accents, and primary CTA buttons Soft teal/mint (#7FD9D4 or similar) as a secondary accent for icons, dividers, and section backgrounds Deep burgundy/maroon (#7A2138 or similar) used sparingly for small accent dots or text highlights Predominantly white and very light gray backgrounds to keep the site feeling clean and spacious, not overwhelming with color Use the pink and teal together the way they appear in the logo (pink for warmth/energy, teal for freshness/cleanliness) Typography: Clean, modern sans-serif for all body text and most headers (Poppins or Inter), confident and professional. Reserve script/handwritten-style font (matching the logo's feel) only for the logo itself and maybe one small accent phrase, like a section tagline, do not use script font for body copy or it'll feel unprofessional. Imagery: Use real, purposeful photography (not illustrations or cartoon icons) that clearly communicates cleaning: a person wiping a clean countertop, sunlight streaming into a tidy living room, neatly made beds, sparkling kitchen surfaces, a cleaner's hand spraying a surface. Avoid generic stock-photo feel, choose images that feel bright, natural, and aspirational (the "after" feeling of a clean home). For icons (service cards, trust badges, etc.), use simple, real line-style icons that clearly represent their meaning (a spray bottle icon for cleaning supplies, a calendar icon for scheduling, a checkmark/shield icon for guarantees, a clock icon for hours), not abstract shapes. Overall layout principle: Generous whitespace between every section, nothing should feel cramped. Use the pink and teal as accents and section dividers rather than large color blocks, let white space do most of the work so the page feels premium and easy to scan. 1.
